Insurance Details

Insurance Component Description
Liability Coverage Covers bodily injury and property damage you cause to others in an accident where you are at fault. It often includes two limits: one for bodily injury per person and one for property damage.
Collision Coverage Pays for repairs to your vehicle if it's damaged in a collision with another vehicle or object, regardless of fault.
Comprehensive Coverage Provides coverage for damage to your vehicle from non-collision incidents, such as theft, vandalism, fire, hail, or natural disasters.
Uninsured Motorist Coverage Protects you if you're in an accident with a driver who has no insurance or insufficient coverage to pay for your damages. Includes both bodily injury and property damage coverage.
Underinsured Motorist Coverage Similar to uninsured motorist coverage, it kicks in if the at-fault driver has insurance, but their coverage is inadequate to cover your expenses.
Medical Payments (MedPay) Covers medical expenses for you and your passengers, regardless of fault in an accident.
Personal Injury Protection (PIP) Similar to MedPay, but it often includes broader coverage, such as lost wages and essential services. It's available in "no-fault" insurance states.
Gap Insurance Covers the difference between the actual cash value of your vehicle and the amount you still owe on a car loan or lease if your car is totaled.
Rental Reimbursement Pays for the cost of renting a replacement vehicle if your car is in the shop due to a covered claim./td>
Towing and Roadside Assistance Provides assistance if your vehicle breaks down, including towing, jump-starts, tire changes, and fuel delivery.
